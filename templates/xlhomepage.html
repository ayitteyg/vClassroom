Named Ranges

We normally performs some operations on certain columns with data in the spreed sheet. Example: we have the following sales data and would like to sum the sales at cell C5 . We would usally go the cell C5 and enter =SUM(B5:B16). This is simple right, but what if we name the range we want to sum as sales_data and refer to it always.


HOW?

Select the data range you want to sum, ie: B5:B16. On your ribbon, go to Formulas> Named Manager > New. give a name to your data: like sales_data. click on the reffer to, to see if you selected the right data, then CLOSE. Now in cell C12, enter = sum(sales_data)


Editing

You can change the name later if you wish, by using the edit instead of new. you can increase the range for future entery, for instance, go the Named Manager, select the named range, go to refers to, instead of :B16 , increase it to :B16000. Now any sales you enter within that column range is captured


How it improves your daily task

Assuming you often have to refer to your sales data multiples times in different sheets in your workbook. Naming your data saves your from shuffling between sheets to locate your data and dragging it. Wherever, you are in your workbook, just enter =sum(sales_data)
if your data spans several columns and rows, you can equally name the whole range and refer to it anywhere in your workbook for further analysis

Data Validation / Drop down

While working on our spreed sheets, sometimes we would prefer getting some sort of drop down list of items so we can select from, or probably restrict a particular cell or range to accept only a specific type of data. This is where data validation comes in handy.

For instance you have a list of customer at range B4:B10. you want to assign a customer a certain locations, say Accra,Kumasi,Tema. you want to avoid copy and paste or typing every time. What do you do?


HOW?

select the cell or range you want to get the drop down list, in this case cell E5:E7, On the ribbon, go to
Data
Data Validation
Select List from Allow
click on source, move to customer list and select range B4:B10
If you had name your range Then at source, you could enter =(named_range) and that should work as well


Editing

To clear the data validation, following the procedure above, then click clear all from the dialog box, you can check the box to clear from all cells / range with that same settings.


How it improves your daily task

Aside giving you a handy drop down tool to select from, it guards you against certain unintended errors which may have gone unnoticed.
For instance try to assign Accra to daniel by typing daneil instead of daniel. It throws an error and would not allow it. you can try editting the error message at the data validation dialog box, Error Alert. to suit your own style

Conditional formatting | highights

While working on data in our spread sheet, another great tool at our disposal is conditional formatting. There are several options we can choose from, but my focus will be a simple highighting. Simple but very powerful to easily identity patterns in our data.

For instance you have data on credit sales and would like to quickly highlight the values values based on certain criteria
1. All sales above 5,000 should be in red
2. All sales between 5,000 and 2,000 should be in yellow
3. All sales below 2,000 should be in green

Ooh, you can decide to do that manually, but certainly not when you have 1,000+ data. Just apply conditional formatting to that range of data or the whole column and you headache is gone. conditional-formatting is dynamic, therefore the highlighter will change based on the data entered.


HOW?

select the cell,range or column that has the values or you would like to enter the values. On the ribbon, go the conditional formatting, and select your option. In the case the first option. Now this would be the logic, Anytime I enter a value in the range or column,
If it's greater than 5,000 highight it as red
Else if it's between 5,000 and 2,000 highlight it as Yellow
Else if it's less than 2,000 highight it as green.

You are now testing 3 conditions. So you first choose greater than, then enter 5,000 and the color code, Ok.
choose between, then enter 2,000 and 5,000 then your color code, Ok
choose less than, then enter 2,000 then your color code, Ok


Say you have this data and what to apply conditional-formatting, select range C5:C16 and apply the rules as above

IF

the IF statement is one of the most powerful logical statement. It check a condition and returns a value you want if the condition is met or not met. The IF logical test comes in handy when you would want a certain feedback / summary / or answer based on certain conditions Or you would want to assign a certain value to something based on a condition.


syntax?

The syntax for the IF functions is [ IF(logical_test, [value_if_true], [value_if_false]) ]


Ex1

if my total credit sales for this month is more than GHc 100,000, write 'Limit is exceeded', if not, write 'Within Limit'

Enter this formular in the cell you want the result in

=IF(SUM(G5:G16)>G18,"Limit exceeded","Within Limit")


Try changing the limit or increase some of the credit sales value to see the effect

Ex2

You gave 5,000 credit each to some customers as loan and they are currently paying thier debt. However, you want to group them in two categories in order to take further decision on them.
The idea is any customer whose total payment so far is more than 80% of his / her debt should be rated as A, if not rate that customer as B

Solution!!

There are several ways to look at this, but basically, you could just divide total payment by the loan amount and multiply by 0.8 to get your value

Enter this formular in the firt cell M6 and drag it over the range

=IF((L6/$L$3)>=$M$3,"A","B")


If you had error while dragging it, then probably you forgot to lock the cell which contains the loan amount or the percentage. To lock the cell, hit on where you entered the first formular, then on the formular bar, click on the that particular cell refference in the formular.
In my case L3 for the loan and M3 for the percentage. Once you've cliked on it press F4 on the keyboard to lock it. There will be a seperate tutotial on cell referencing later.
